Taylor Series Expansion

The Taylor series is a method named after Brook Taylor

Approximate the value of a function \( f(x+\Delta x) \), if I know the value of \( f(x) \) and the value of the derivatives \( f'(x) \), \( f''(x) \), \( f'''(x) \), \( \cdots \)

Taylor Series

Infinite sum that converges to the true value

\[ f(x + \Delta x) = f(x) + \frac{f'(a)}{1!}(x-\Delta x) + \frac{f''(a)}{2!}(x-\Delta x)^2 + + \frac{f'''(a)}{2!}(x-\Delta x)^3 + ... \]

Taylor Expansion

The denser your points are, the better the approximation is going to be

The weight of the derivatives scales with \( \frac{1}{l!} \), where \( l \) is the level of the approximation (Taylor series)

The influence of higher levels is decreasing exponentially, so the more inportant are the first few levels

Since, it takes more computation the more levels we have, we usually only use levels less than 5
